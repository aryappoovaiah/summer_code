Interview Tips

Research the company thoroughly before the interview.

Arrive 10-15 minutes early for in-person interviews.

Dress professionally, even for virtual interviews.

Practice your answers but keep them natural, not rehearsed.

Ask Post-Grad Questions

Get personalized advice about graduate school, job searching, and career planning.

AI Suggestions

Question:

"How soon should I start applying for jobs before graduation?"

Answer:

For most industries, you should begin your job search 6-9 months before graduation. Many large companies recruit early for entry-level positions. For smaller companies, 3-4 months before may be sufficient. Start by:

  • Updating your resume and LinkedIn profile
  • Attending career fairs in the fall semester
  • Networking with alumni in your field
  • Researching companies you're interested in

Question:

"Is graduate school worth it for my field?"

Answer:

This depends on your career goals and field. For some professions (like academia, law, or medicine), advanced degrees are essential. For others, experience may be more valuable. Consider:

  • Career paths of professionals in your desired role
  • Earning potential with vs. without a graduate degree
  • Opportunity cost of time and tuition
  • Whether your employer might help fund further education

Saved Questions

  • What's your favorite way to spend a weekend?
  • If you could instantly become an expert in something, what would it be?
  • What's something you're looking forward to this semester?

Social Tips

Begin with open-ended questions that can't be answered with just "yes" or "no." Comment on your shared environment ("This lecture hall is huge!") or ask for opinions ("What did you think of that assignment?").

Repeat the person's name when you meet them ("Nice to meet you, Sarah"). Associate their name with something memorable (Sarah who wears sunglasses). Use their name a few times in conversation.

Approach with open body language. Listen first to understand the topic. Wait for a natural pause to contribute. You can say, "That's interesting - I was just learning about that in my [class]."
